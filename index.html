Where am I Going?

I am over a decade into my career and looking to move into the software industry. When you are 18 years old and you pick your career, you really don't know what you want. I love managing relationships with people and departments, technical projects, programs, whatever it might be. What I manage might change, but the foundation is always the same. So I am looking to take those skills somewhere that better aligns with my interests. Early in my career I made several VBA macro's for large groups of people. Quickly you learn that people do not use Excel exactly the way you do. In fact, some do things in a really weird way. I might be able to get Excel to do anything short of changing the physical world, but I may never fully know how to people proof it so it never breaks. I also make amateur android apps, and those obstacles are more around how people know what to do without you having to explain it. I have a lot to learn, but I also have a deep appreciation for the field. It is not easy, and it is certainly not kind. I am looking to combine my love and interest in software development, with my skills as a project manager. I am a quick learner, and excited for my next opportunity.

Career Highlights

Engineering Project Coordination

Coordinated engineering activities to get FAA certification as efficiently as possible. In this role, I identified needs and developed tools to prioritize tasks with complex relationships. Ownership of these tools were handed off to the software team where they were made more robust and are still in use today.

Product Support Specialist

What doesn't a Product Support Specialist do? Maybe the most fun job of my career. Anywhere from finding ways to automate aircraft spec sheets, managing product improvements with outside vendors, to pulling out all stops to make sure deliveries happen on time. I even would help customers determine if their bike would fit in one of our smaller airplanes. It was a small team with very low priority, but the work was just as important as the higher priority tasks.

Deputy Program Manager

After spending 7 years in aviation, I joined The Spaceship Company which is the company that designed and manufactured the spaceships for Virgin Galactic. I was brought on board to be part of the two person team that would establish the Customer Support organization. Nothing like starting with a blank slate and getting to build an organization into something of value. As products transfered ownership from one business to the other there was no shortage of obstacles to address. Anywhere from having to build up better relationships with the team, to setting them up for success with the tools they would need to do regular maintenance. This was all uncharted territory for the company.

Senior Project Manager

In 2019 I joined the Kansas City National Security Campus managed by Honeywell. I received a Q level security clearance, and managed multiple projects supporting the test equipment needs of the facility. I worked on a team of 20-30 technical project managers, until I was given the opportunity to manage the team. Earned Value Management is the core methodology behind how we manage our projects. My team manages over 400 different projects, and acts as the liasons between the Program Management Office and Engineering as well as managing the scope, schedule, and cost for each project.
